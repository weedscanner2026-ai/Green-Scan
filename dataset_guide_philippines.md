## Collection Tips for Philippine Climate

### Best Time to Collect
- **Early morning (6-9 AM)**: Good lighting, plants fresh
- **Late afternoon (4-6 PM)**: Softer light, avoid harsh midday sun
- **After rain**: Plants are clean and vibrant
- **Avoid**: Heavy rain or very windy conditions

### Seasonal Considerations
- **Rainy season (June-November)**: Most weeds abundant and green
- **Dry season (December-May)**: Some weeds still present, may look stressed
- **Collect year-round**: Get variety in plant conditions

### Safety Tips
- Wear long pants and closed shoes
- Use gloves for spiny plants (makahiya, talahib)
- Watch for ants and insects
- Avoid private property without permission
- Be careful near roads

## Image Collection Guidelines

### Minimum Images Per Weed
- **Start with 5 main weeds**: 200 images each = 1,000 total
- **Add more weeds later**: 150-200 images each
- **Target**: 15 weed types × 200 images = 3,000 images

### Photo Diversity (CRITICAL for accuracy)

#### 1. Growth Stages
- Young seedlings
- Mature plants
- Flowering stage
- With seeds/fruits

#### 2. Lighting (Philippine conditions)
- Bright tropical sun
- Cloudy/overcast
- Morning light
- Afternoon light
- Partial shade

#### 3. Backgrounds
- Soil/dirt
- Grass/lawn
- Concrete/pavement
- Gravel
- Mixed with other plants
- Rice field (for teki, crabgrass)

#### 4. Angles
- Top view (most important)
- 45-degree angle
- Side view
- Close-up of leaves
- Close-up of flowers
- Whole plant with surroundings

#### 5. Phone Camera Settings
- Use regular camera mode (not portrait)
- Tap to focus on the weed
- Take multiple shots of same plant
- Use good lighting
- Clean camera lens first!

## Organizing Your Photos

### After Collection
1. Transfer photos to computer
2. Create folders by weed name
3. Review each photo:
   - Delete blurry images
   - Delete duplicates
   - Verify correct weed identification
4. Rename files systematically:
   ```
   crabgrass_001.jpg
   crabgrass_002.jpg
   morsikos_flowering_001.jpg
   ```

### Quality Check
- ✓ In focus
- ✓ Weed clearly visible
- ✓ Good lighting
- ✓ Correct identification
- ✗ Too blurry
- ✗ Wrong plant
- ✗ Too dark/bright

## Pro Tips for Philippine Context

1. **Start with easy weeds**: Morsikos and paragis are everywhere
2. **Use barangay areas**: Roadsides, vacant lots (public areas)
3. **Ask permission**: For private gardens or farms
4. **Involve community**: Ask neighbors to help identify
5. **Document location**: Note where you found each weed
6. **Weather**: Best during "amihan" (dry season) for clear photos
7. **Time efficient**: Collect 2-3 weed types per outing

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ All photos from same location
❌ All photos same angle
❌ Only mature plants (no young stages)
❌ Only perfect specimens
❌ Poor lighting (too dark/bright)
❌ Wrong identification
❌ Too few images per weed

✓ Diverse locations
✓ Multiple angles
✓ Different growth stages
✓ Include imperfect plants
✓ Good varied lighting
✓ Verify identification
✓ 200+ images per weed
